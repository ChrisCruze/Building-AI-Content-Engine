# AI Content Templates Guide

This document provides reusable **formats** and concrete **examples** that operationalize the system at scale across platforms and contributors. Templates translate the [[Growth-Strategy]] and [[Agent-Framework]] into actionable content structures that ensure consistency, quality, and efficiency.

> **Important**: Templates are only effective when backed by solid strategy and technical architecture. Without the foundation provided by [[Growth-Strategy]] and [[Agent-Framework]], templates will produce fast but low-quality output.

## Template Usage Overview

### Purpose of Templates

Templates serve multiple functions:
1. **Consistency**: Ensure all content follows proven structures
2. **Efficiency**: Speed up content generation with reusable formats
3. **Quality**: Guide content creation toward best practices
4. **Scalability**: Enable content production at volume
5. **Brand Alignment**: Maintain brand voice and positioning

### When to Use Templates

- **Standard Content**: Routine blog posts, social posts, newsletters
- **High-Volume Production**: When generating multiple pieces quickly
- **New Contributors**: Onboarding and training
- **Consistency Requirements**: When brand consistency is critical

### When to Deviate from Templates

- **Unique Opportunities**: Special events or campaigns
- **Experimental Content**: Testing new formats or approaches
- **Crisis Communications**: Situations requiring custom messaging
- **High-Profile Content**: Major announcements or strategic content

### Template Customization

Templates are **starting points**, not rigid constraints:
- Adapt templates to specific topics and audiences
- Modify structure when it improves engagement
- Combine elements from multiple templates
- Iterate based on performance data

## Blog Content Templates

### Standard Blog Article Template

```
# [Engaging, SEO-Optimized Title]

> [Hook: 1-2 sentences that capture attention and promise value]

## Introduction
[2-3 paragraphs that establish context, introduce the topic, and preview what readers will learn]

## [Main Section 1: Core Concept]
[3-5 paragraphs explaining the main concept with examples and insights]

### [Subsection: Supporting Detail]
[2-3 paragraphs with specific details, examples, or data]

## [Main Section 2: Application]
[3-5 paragraphs showing how to apply the concept or why it matters]

### [Subsection: Real-World Example]
[2-3 paragraphs with concrete examples or case studies]

## [Main Section 3: Actionable Takeaways]
[3-5 paragraphs with practical advice, tips, or next steps]

## Conclusion
[2-3 paragraphs that summarize key points, reinforce value, and include call-to-action]

---

**Ready to connect over food?** [Join our next dining event] or [Sign up for our newsletter] to stay updated on upcoming experiences.
```

### Blog Content Best Practices

- **Headlines**: Clear, benefit-focused, include keywords
- **Opening**: Hook within first 100 words
- **Structure**: Use headers to break up content, make scannable
- **Length**: 800-2000 words for SEO and engagement
- **Links**: Include internal links to platform and related content
- **Images**: Use relevant images with descriptive alt text
- **CTAs**: Clear, specific calls-to-action

### Social Media Best Practices

- **Visual First**: Use high-quality images or videos
- **Authentic Voice**: Match brand voice but be platform-appropriate
- **Engagement**: Ask questions, encourage interaction
- **Timing**: Post when audience is most active
- **Hashtags**: Use platform-appropriate number and mix
- **Links**: Use link in bio for Instagram, direct links elsewhere
- **Consistency**: Post regularly, maintain brand voice

## Newsletter Templates

### Weekly Newsletter Template

```
Subject: [Engaging subject line: 40-50 characters]

Preview Text: [Compelling preview: 80-100 characters]

---

[Header: Logo and navigation]

# [Newsletter Title/Theme]

> [Opening hook: 1-2 sentences that set the tone]

## Featured Story
[Main article or highlight: 3-5 paragraphs]

[Read more link]

## This Week's Highlights
[Bulleted list of 3-5 key items]
- [Event or story 1]
- [Event or story 2]
- [Event or story 3]

## Upcoming Events
[Event listings with dates, locations, links]

## Community Spotlight
[User story or community highlight: 2-3 paragraphs]

## Quick Tips
[Short tips or insights: 2-3 bullet points]

---

[Footer: Social links, unsubscribe, contact info]
```

### Event-Focused Newsletter Template

```
Subject: [Event Name] - Join Us This [Day/Week]

Preview Text: [Event hook and date]

---

[Header]

# [Event Name] - [Date]

> [Event description and value proposition]

## What to Expect
[Event details: 2-3 paragraphs]
- Location: [Venue]
- Date: [Date and time]
- Theme: [Event theme or cuisine]

## Why You Should Join
[Benefits and value: 2-3 paragraphs]

## Meet Your Host
[Host introduction: 1-2 paragraphs]

## How to Sign Up
[Clear sign-up instructions and link]

## Can't Make This One?
[Upcoming events preview]

---

[Footer]
```

### Newsletter Best Practices

- **Subject Lines**: Clear, benefit-focused, create curiosity
- **Preview Text**: Compelling snippet that encourages opens
- **Structure**: Scannable with clear sections
- **Length**: 500-1500 words total
- **Visuals**: Include images but optimize for email clients
- **CTAs**: Clear, prominent calls-to-action
- **Mobile**: Ensure responsive design
- **Personalization**: Use recipient name and preferences when possible

## Customization and Iteration Guidance

### Customizing Templates

1. **Audience Adaptation**
   - Adjust tone for different audience segments
   - Modify examples to be relevant
   - Change CTAs to match audience goals

2. **Topic Adaptation**
   - Adjust structure for different content types
   - Modify examples to fit topic
   - Change emphasis based on content goals

3. **Channel Adaptation**
   - Adjust length for platform constraints
   - Modify format for platform requirements
   - Change engagement strategies per platform

### Iteration Based on Performance

1. **Track Metrics**
   - Engagement rates
   - Conversion rates
   - Time on page/read time
   - Social shares

2. **Identify Patterns**
   - What structures perform best?
   - Which CTAs drive action?
   - What topics resonate?

3. **Test Variations**
   - A/B test template variations
   - Test different structures
   - Experiment with formats

4. **Refine Templates**
   - Update templates based on learnings
   - Document what works
   - Share insights with team

### Template Maintenance

- **Regular Review**: Quarterly review of template performance
- **Version Control**: Track template versions and changes
- **Documentation**: Document why templates work
- **Team Input**: Gather feedback from content creators
- **Evolution**: Update templates as strategy and performance evolve
