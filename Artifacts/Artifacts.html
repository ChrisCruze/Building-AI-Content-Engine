User Activation Insights

Uncertainty, not social anxiety, is the primary barrier to activation for dormant users

01

The Stall (Months 0-6)

"I didn't avoid the app; I avoided the uncertainty."

Users engage in "anticipatory anxiety," mentally filling in gaps with worst-case scenarios (e.g., awkwardness, isolation). The app is downloaded, but the ambiguity of the actual experience prevents RSVP.

02

The Guilt Loop

"The longer I waited, the bigger it felt."

Inaction transforms into shame over time. The app icon becomes a reminder of a failed social goal. Users consider deleting the app to remove the psychological pressure, creating a high risk of churn.

03

The Emotional Pivot

"It wasn't confidence; it was a bad Tuesday."

Activation is rarely driven by sudden confidence. It is triggered by a negative emotional state (boredom, acute loneliness) that outweighs the fear of the unknown. The RSVP is an impulsive experiment, not a lifestyle change.

04

The Reality Check

"The scariest part was the walk there."

Anxiety is front-loaded, peaking just before arrival. Once present, the reality of "normal human interaction" dissolves the fear. The barrier was never the event itself, but the imagined version of it.

Strategic Implication
Shift messaging from "Inspiration" to "Demystification." To activate dormant users, marketing should explicitly normalize hesitation ("It's okay to come alone") and provide granular details about the event mechanics to remove the uncertainty gap, rather than focusing solely on the social benefits.
